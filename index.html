Can a Scientist Believe in the Resurrection?

by Professor Ian Hutchinson

May 2016

Three hypotheses

I'm a professor of nuclear science and engineering at MIT, and today, I am celebrating the resurrection of Jesus. So are dozens of my colleagues. How can this be?

Hypothesis one: We're not talking about a literal resurrection. Perhaps it is just an inspiring myth that served to justify the propagation of Jesus' exalted ethical teachings. A literal resurrection contradicts the known laws of nature. Maybe scientists can celebrate the idea of Jesus's spirit living on, while his body remained in the grave. But the first disciples attested to a physical resurrection. How could an untruth logically support high moral character? How could it have sustained the apostles through the extremes of persecution they experienced founding Christianity? And is celebrating a myth consistent with scientific integrity?
